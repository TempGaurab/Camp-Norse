Summer Day Camp Memories

Camp Norse inspired and challenged the next generation of thinkers and leaders while offering them a glimpse into the opportunities for their future. Nearly 200 campers participated in themed camps between June-August, experiencing hands-on activities, meeting NKU students, and engaging in interactive learning on NKU's campus.

June 9-13

Board Game Design

Grades 4-6

Students studied, analyzed, and created their own games based on a topic of their choosing. The game development process deepened abstract and critical thinking skills while furthering literacy, art, problem solving, research, and cooperation skills. Students created their own Roll & Move games and presented them to peers.

June 16-18

Life Skills Lab

Grades 4-6

A holistic approach to prepare young folks for the challenges of adulthood. Covering twelve essential modules, including financial literacy, job application strategies, and effective communication. The program integrated theoretical knowledge with hands-on training to empower students with comprehensive tools for successful adulting.

Vikings & Valkyries

Grades 2-3

Campers explored the history of the people who inspired NKU's mascot Victor E. Viking. They made Viking age crafts, played games, learned about Norse mythology, and created their own stories inspired by the Norse. This was an adventure through ancient Scandinavian culture and legends.

June 30 - July 3

America250: Kentucky's Role

FULL

All Grades

Campers unlocked history through games as we celebrated 250 years of America! Using game-based learning, they explored the American Revolution, women's suffrage, the Underground Railroad, and the Mississippi Flood of 1927. The week concluded with presentations of original games inspired by Kentucky history. Funded in part by the Kentucky Historical Society.

July 21-25

Science of the Human Body

Half Day - Morning

Grades 4-6

The camp dived into the basics of bodily functions including mechanics of movement, senses like sight and sound, and internal systems working together. Students learned through activities like measuring heart rate after exercise and calculating energy expenditure. They also learned about proper nutrition and maintaining lifelong health and fitness.

July 21-25

Science of Superpowers

Half Day - Afternoon

Grades 2-3

Students explored how famous superheroes modeled their identities after creatures with unique abilities using engineering and design. With 12 hands-on lessons, biology, engineering, and technology concepts were discussed through comic book heroes. Campers also identified real-life community heroes and what makes someone a true hero.
